Why NullShield?

Most cybersecurity firms understand IT. We understand OT — the operational technology inside substations, water treatment plants, and factory floors. The protocols are different. The stakes are higher. An intrusion here doesn't mean leaked data; it can mean a city without power.

Our platform works passively alongside your existing infrastructure — no production disruption, no injected packets. We deploy, baseline, and go live within days, not months.

What we protect

Power & Energy
Grid Defense Platform

Protects generation, transmission, and distribution assets from state-sponsored intrusion, ransomware, and supply-chain attacks. Built around NERC CIP requirements, with native support for IEC 61850 and DNP3 protocols.

NERC CIP IEC 61850 DNP3 Substation monitoring
Water & Wastewater
AquaGuard Module

Chemical dosing systems and SCADA networks are high-value targets. AquaGuard enforces operational integrity and alerts on any unauthorized command injection — helping utilities meet AWIA 2018 compliance requirements.

AWIA 2018 SCADA Dosing integrity Remote access logging
Industrial Manufacturing
FactoryShield ICS

Production-floor OT environments demand near-zero-latency detection. FactoryShield works passively — monitoring Modbus, PROFINET, and EtherNet/IP traffic without injecting a single packet or interrupting operations.

Modbus PROFINET EtherNet/IP PLC/HMI inventory

Built by people who
know the risk

NullShield was founded in 2012 in San Antonio, Texas, by former ICS engineers and federal security researchers who saw a gap no one was filling: real operational technology security with the depth it deserves.

We protect the power behind your hospital, the water in your pipes, and the factories that make what you use every day. When those systems fail, people get hurt. We exist to make sure they don't.

Our team holds active clearances, has responded to incidents at over 80 critical facilities across the United States, and continues to publish research on emerging OT threats through our partnerships with CISA and the Department of Energy.
